File CIT Lawsuit (Critical Step)
Per McCune Wright research: the customs protest pathway (CBP Form 19) is likely INADEQUATE for IEEPA refunds. The real pathway is filing a lawsuit in the U.S. Court of International Trade. The government has agreed to refund CIT plaintiffs following a final, unappealable decision. If you're not a CIT plaintiff, the January 8, 2026 DOJ agreement doesn't cover you. This is the most important legal cost in the stack. Contingency firms: 20‚Äì25% of recovery, no upfront cost. Hourly firms: $350‚Äì$650/hr for trade attorneys. Wait for Reliquidation + ACH Refund
CBP processes reliquidation orders from the CIT. Refunds issued electronically via ACH (all refunds are now electronic per Feb 6, 2026 CBP rule). Timeline: unknown. Government is fighting it. Realistic estimate per every expert: 2‚Äì5 years from today. The January 8 DOJ stipulation gives CIT plaintiffs the clearest path ‚Äî the government already said it won't contest the CIT's reliquidation authority. The Flexport Fee Model ‚Äî What It Actually Means

Flexport charges 10% of the refund amount + 3.5% of the duty paid per entry for filing. Here's how that stacks against your deal economics and whether it's competitive.

Flexport Fee Structure
10% of Refund + 3.5% Filing Fee
10%
Of total refund received
Flexport's success fee ‚Äî only charged if/when the refund actually comes in. On a $300K claim: $30,000 to Flexport.

3.5%
Of duties paid per entry (filing fee)
Upfront filing/admin fee per entry. On a $300K claim: $10,500 upfront regardless of outcome. This is where Flexport makes money even if the refund fails.
Example: $300,000 refund claim Flexport filing fee (3.5%): -$10,500 (upfront) Flexport success fee (10%): -$30,000 (at refund) Total Flexport takes: -$40,500 Importer nets: $259,500 vs. doing nothing: $300,000 vs. selling claim at 45¬¢: $135,000 (now) vs. contingency law firm: ~$234,000 (3-5 yrs) Flexport middle position: More than selling, less than DIY law Paid over time, not upfront (except 3.5%)
Note: Flexport is positioning this as a "managed service" ‚Äî they handle the PSC/protest filing and work with CBP directly. They're NOT filing CIT lawsuits, which per McCune Wright is the more reliable path to recovery.
Flexport's model works across multiple companies simultaneously since it's volume-driven. The 3.5% filing fee makes it profitable even with low refund rates. The 10% success fee is the real margin driver at scale.
üí°
Can you replicate the Flexport model across multiple businesses?
Yes ‚Äî and it's actually more compelling than Flexport's because you can combine filing services with the option/upside split structure. Offer importers: "We file your claim (3.5% fee), take a 10% success fee, AND give you cash now via an option on 50% of the claim." The importer gets money today AND keeps upside. You get the filing fee revenue plus the investor pays you a finder's fee. Three revenue streams from one transaction.

The Legal Facts You Need to Know Cold

üö®
CBP Form 19 protest is NOT the right path for IEEPA refunds
Per McCune Wright (trade litigation firm): "The customs protest pathway has been determined to be an inadequate pathway for IEEPA tariff recovery. Importers who assume they can file a protest and wait are likely to lose their claims entirely." The real path is filing a CIT lawsuit. The Jan 8 DOJ stipulation only covers CIT plaintiffs.
‚è∞
180-day protest window is ticking ‚Äî but CIT has no stated deadline yet
Liquidated entries: protest within 180 days of liquidation (CBP Form 19 ‚Äî limited use, but file anyway as "belt and suspenders"). CIT lawsuit: no stated deadline yet but file ASAP to be a named plaintiff under the Jan 8 DOJ stipulation. Unliquidated entries: PSC route via ACE portal, 300 days from entry date or 15 days before liquidation.
üìã
Claim assignment is legally possible ‚Äî but must be structured carefully
The refund goes to whoever is the Importer of Record. To transfer the economic benefit, the claim purchase agreement must assign the right to receive and direct the refund. Orrick (Pasacreta's team) and Foley & Lardner are the two law firms most active in structuring these. Expect $3K‚Äì$8K for first-deal legal + $500‚Äì$1,500/repeat on a template. The assignment must survive potential government challenge ‚Äî do not use a generic purchase agreement.
